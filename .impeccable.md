## Design Context

### Users
Keystone serves cryptographers, security engineers, and technical evaluators who need to assess post-quantum cryptography, hybrid encryption, and quantum workload behavior with confidence. They arrive focused and skeptical: they want evidence, traceability, reproducibility, and a credible path from evaluation to adoption.

### Brand Personality
Monolithic, precise, resilient. Keystone should feel like infrastructure: compressed, engineered, trustworthy, and built for what comes next. The brand should avoid hype, mysticism, startup gloss, and decorative cyberpunk tropes.

### Aesthetic Direction
The landing page should move toward the supplied Keystone brand board: a monolithic hidden-K/KS glyph language, refined technical typography, blueprint-like structure, disciplined monochrome surfaces, and restrained blue accents. The full landing page is the immediate scope, while the resulting brand system should be strong enough to influence the product UI later. The preferred theme is light-first with deliberate dark technical bands, echoing the board's white identity-system field and dark rationale/specification panels.

### Design Principles
1. Make the mark structural: use the Keystone glyph and its geometry as a layout and identity device, not just a small nav icon.
2. Let precision carry the design: clear spacing, exact rules, measured type, and traceable data should create trust.
3. Use color rarely: rely on off-white, graphite, slate, and black; reserve blueprint blue for hierarchy, links, evidence, and key actions.
4. Keep the voice factual and compressed: no inflated claims, no quantum mysticism, no generic marketing drama.
5. Preserve product evidence: screenshots, algorithm data, checksums, and source links remain central because the audience evaluates proof.
